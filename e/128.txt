Since we already have three queens, we don't want to consider the case where we can get a pair of queens, being as this is clearly impossible. The total number of ways we can get any two cards is 49 C 2, or 1176. Therefore, our probability that we get a pair in any two places of those four cards is 432/1176, or 18/49. However, we aren't done yet because we have to make sure that we don't get another queen or two more of the same previous two cards that we drew on the remaining two cards, because either case would result in four of a kind.
